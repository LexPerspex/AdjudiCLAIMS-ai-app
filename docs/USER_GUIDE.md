## 1. What is AdjudiCLAIMS?

AdjudiCLAIMS is an AI-powered claims management information tool built specifically for California Workers' Compensation professionals. It is designed to help you work more accurately, meet every regulatory deadline, and stay in full compliance with California law.

### What AdjudiCLAIMS Does

- Provides factual summaries of claim documents with citations to source material
- Calculates statutory benefits using published formulas (Temporary Disability rates, payment schedules, death benefits)
- Tracks every regulatory deadline across your caseload and alerts you before deadlines approach
- Classifies and organizes uploaded claim documents automatically
- Matches treatment requests against MTUS/ACOEM guidelines for utilization review reference
- Generates standardized correspondence letters populated with your claim data
- Guides you through 20 step-by-step decision workflows for common claims processes
- Prepares factual claim summaries when you need to refer a matter to defense counsel
- Tracks liens and compares billed amounts against the Official Medical Fee Schedule (OMFS)

### What AdjudiCLAIMS Is NOT

AdjudiCLAIMS is not a legal advisor. It does not provide legal advice, legal analysis, or legal conclusions. Under California Business and Professions Code section 6125, only licensed attorneys may practice law. Claims examiners handle factual and administrative work; legal questions must go to defense counsel.

This means AdjudiCLAIMS will never tell you:

- Whether to accept or deny a claim
- What a claim is "worth"
- Whether coverage applies to a particular situation
- What legal strategy to pursue
- Whether to settle or how much to offer

When you ask a question that crosses into legal territory, AdjudiCLAIMS will tell you directly and help you prepare a factual summary to send to your defense counsel.

### A Day in the Life: How AdjudiCLAIMS Helps You

Here is a typical scenario showing how AdjudiCLAIMS supports your daily work:

You arrive Monday morning and check your dashboard. AdjudiCLAIMS shows you that Claim #WC-2026-0431 has a 15-day acknowledgment deadline in RED — only 2 days remaining. You open the claim, generate an acknowledgment letter using the letter template, and mark the deadline as met. The system logs the completion and updates your compliance metrics.

Next, you see a new claim assigned to you over the weekend. You open it and notice the system has already generated all regulatory deadlines and the 10-item investigation checklist. You start the "New Claim Intake" workflow, which walks you through each step: logging receipt, verifying the DWC-1, sending the acknowledgment, notifying the employer, and initiating your investigation.

A medical report was uploaded to one of your other claims. The AI has already classified it as a Medical Report and auto-completed the "Medical Records Requested" checklist item. You open the AI chat and ask: "What did the treating physician diagnose?" The AI searches the document, finds the diagnosis on page 3, and tells you: "The treating physician diagnosed a lumbar disc herniation at L4-L5 with left lower extremity radiculopathy. Source: Dr. Smith Medical Report, page 3, paragraph 2."

Later, you wonder whether you should deny a questionable claim. You type: "Should I deny this claim?" The AI classifies this as RED zone and responds: "This question requires legal analysis. Whether to accept or deny a claim involves legal judgment about compensability that only your defense counsel can provide. Would you like me to prepare a factual summary for your attorney?" You click yes, describe the legal issue, and AdjudiCLAIMS generates a structured summary you can forward to counsel.

This is how AdjudiCLAIMS works: it handles the factual, arithmetic, and procedural work so you can focus on managing your claims. It keeps you on track with deadlines, helps you find information fast, and directs you to counsel when a legal question arises.

### The Glass Box Philosophy

The name "Glass Box" reflects our core belief: you should always be able to see inside the system that helps you make decisions. Every output from AdjudiCLAIMS includes:

- **Citations** — Every factual statement references the source document, page, or data point it came from.
- **Statutory authority** — Every deadline, calculation, and workflow step cites the specific California statute or regulation that requires it.
- **Explanations** — AdjudiCLAIMS does not just tell you what to do; it explains why the regulation exists and what the consequences of non-compliance are.

AdjudiCLAIMS is not just a tool. It is also your training program. The education system is woven into every feature so that you learn California Workers' Compensation regulations as you work. New examiners see more educational context; experienced examiners can dismiss introductory definitions once they have mastered them. But the core regulatory explanations are always visible to everyone, because compliance is not something you outgrow.

## 2. Getting Started

### Logging In

1. Open your web browser and navigate to your organization's AdjudiCLAIMS URL.
2. Enter your email address and password.
3. If this is your first login, you will be directed to the mandatory training program before you can access the full system.

### First-Time Setup: Mandatory Training

Before you can use AdjudiCLAIMS, you must complete four training modules. This requirement comes from 10 CCR 2695.6, which requires every insurer to adopt and communicate minimum training standards to all claims agents and adjusters.

The four modules cover:

- **Module 1:** Understanding the claims examination role, key regulatory concepts, and how AdjudiCLAIMS supports your work
- **Module 2:** How to use the AI chat, what the GREEN/YELLOW/RED zone system means, and why certain questions are redirected to defense counsel
- **Module 3:** Regulatory deadlines, investigation requirements, and how the system tracks compliance
- **Module 4:** Document management, benefit calculations, and decision workflows

Each module includes an assessment. You must pass all four assessments to unlock full access to the system. If you do not pass an assessment on the first attempt, you can review the material and try again.

Once you pass all four modules, your education profile is updated and the training gate is lifted. You will have full access to all features appropriate to your role.

### The Dashboard

After completing training, you will see your main dashboard. The dashboard shows:

- **Your assigned claims** — A list of claims assigned to you, sorted by most recent activity
- **Upcoming deadlines** — Regulatory deadlines approaching across your caseload, sorted by urgency (most urgent first)
- **Investigation progress** — Claims with incomplete investigation items
- **Compliance metrics** — Your personal deadline adherence rate and other compliance indicators

### Understanding Your Role

AdjudiCLAIMS has three user roles. Your role determines what you can see and do in the system.

**Claims Examiner**
- See only claims assigned to you
- Use AI chat, benefit calculator, deadline tracking, investigation checklists, and workflows for your claims
- Upload and view documents on your claims
- Receive educational content and complete training modules
- View your own compliance metrics

**Claims Supervisor**
- See all claims in your organization
- Everything an examiner can do, plus team oversight
- View compliance metrics for all examiners on your team
- See per-examiner deadline adherence and UPL compliance breakdowns
- Review training completion status across your team

**Claims Administrator**
- See all claims in your organization
- Everything a supervisor can do, plus organizational management
- View organization-wide compliance reports
- See DOI audit readiness scores
- Access compliance reporting tools for audit preparation

## 3. Claims Management

### Creating a New Claim

To create a new claim:

1. Select "New Claim" from your dashboard.
2. Enter the required information:
   - **Claim number** — Your organization's claim number
   - **Claimant name** — The injured worker's name
   - **Date of injury** — When the injury occurred
   - **Body parts** — Which body parts are injured (you can select multiple)
   - **Employer** — The injured worker's employer
   - **Insurer** — The insurance carrier or self-insured employer
   - **Date received** — When your organization received notice of the claim
3. Submit the claim.

When you create a claim, AdjudiCLAIMS automatically does three things:

- **Generates regulatory deadlines** — The system calculates all required deadlines based on the date received. These include the 15-day acknowledgment deadline, the 40-day determination deadline, the 14-day first TD payment deadline, and others.
- **Creates an investigation checklist** — The system generates a 10-item investigation checklist including three-point contact (injured worker, employer, medical provider), recorded statement, DWC-1 on file, medical records, employer report, index bureau check, AWE verification, and initial reserves.
- **Logs an audit event** — The claim creation is recorded in the immutable audit trail.

The new claim is automatically assigned to you (the creating examiner).

**Practical example:** You receive a DWC-1 form in the mail on March 1, 2026 for an employee named Maria Garcia who injured her right shoulder at work on February 15, 2026. She works for Acme Manufacturing and the insurer is Pacific Mutual. You enter:
- Claim number: WC-2026-0512 (your organization's numbering system)
- Claimant name: Maria Garcia
- Date of injury: 2026-02-15
- Body parts: Right shoulder
- Employer: Acme Manufacturing
- Insurer: Pacific Mutual
- Date received: 2026-03-01

When you submit, AdjudiCLAIMS generates deadlines including: acknowledgment due by March 16 (15 days), determination due by April 10 (40 days), first TD payment due by March 15 (14 days from employer knowledge), and employer notification due by March 16 (15 days). It also generates the full investigation checklist.

### Claim Status Workflow

Every claim moves through a defined set of statuses:

1. **OPEN** — The claim has been received and logged. Investigation has not formally begun.
2. **UNDER INVESTIGATION** — You are actively investigating the claim: making three-point contact, gathering medical records, verifying wages, and determining compensability.
3. **ACCEPTED** — The claim has been accepted as compensable. Benefits are being provided.
4. **DENIED** — The claim has been denied. A denial letter has been issued citing the specific basis for denial.
5. **CLOSED** — All benefits have been paid, all liens resolved, and the file is archived.
6. **REOPENED** — A previously closed claim has been reopened due to new developments (such as new medical evidence, a change in condition, or a WCAB order).

To change a claim's status, open the claim and update the status field. AdjudiCLAIMS will log every status change in the audit trail, including the previous status and the new status.

### Viewing Claim Details

When you open a claim, you will see:

- **Claim header** — Claim number, claimant name, date of injury, employer, insurer, body parts, and current status
- **Key dates** — Date received, date acknowledged, date determined, date closed
- **Financial summary** — Current reserves (indemnity, medical, legal, lien) and total amounts paid (indemnity, medical)
- **Flags** — Whether the claim is litigated, whether the applicant has an attorney, and whether it is a cumulative trauma claim
- **Related information** — Links to the claim's documents, deadlines, investigation checklist, chat sessions, workflows, liens, and generated letters

### Assigning Claims

Claims are assigned to the examiner who creates them. Supervisors and administrators can reassign claims to different examiners as needed.

Examiners can only see and work on claims assigned to them. Supervisors and administrators can see all claims within their organization.

### Claim Flags

Claims can be flagged with important status indicators:

- **Is Litigated** — Set to true when a Workers' Compensation Appeals Board (WCAB) case has been filed. Litigated claims typically require defense counsel involvement and may have different handling requirements.
- **Has Applicant Attorney** — Set to true when the injured worker has retained an attorney. This flag is important because communication protocols change when an applicant is represented (communications may need to go through the attorney).
- **Is Cumulative Trauma** — Set to true for cumulative trauma (CT) claims, where the injury developed over time from repeated exposure rather than a single incident. CT claims have unique investigation and handling requirements (see the Cumulative Trauma Handling workflow in Section 9).

### Financial Tracking

Each claim tracks financial information across four reserve categories and two payment categories:

**Reserve categories (estimated future costs):**
- Indemnity reserves — Expected future indemnity benefit payments (TD, PD)
- Medical reserves — Expected future medical treatment costs
- Legal reserves — Expected defense attorney fees
- Lien reserves — Expected lien resolution costs

**Payment tracking:**
- Total paid indemnity — Cumulative indemnity benefits paid to date
- Total paid medical — Cumulative medical costs paid to date

Reserves should be reviewed regularly and adjusted as the claim develops. The Reserve Setting and Review workflow (Section 9) guides you through this process.

## 4. Document Management

### Uploading Documents

To upload a document to a claim:

1. Open the claim.
2. Navigate to the Documents section.
3. Select "Upload Document."
4. Choose the file from your computer. Supported formats include PDF, images (JPG, PNG, TIFF), and common document formats.
5. Submit the upload.

### Automatic Processing

After you upload a document, AdjudiCLAIMS automatically processes it through several stages:

1. **OCR (Optical Character Recognition)** — The system extracts text from the document using Google Document AI. This works on scanned documents, faxes, and images. The OCR status is tracked for each document: Pending, Processing, Complete, or Failed.

2. **Document Classification** — The AI analyzes the extracted text and classifies the document into one of the following types:
   - DWC-1 Claim Form
   - Medical Report
   - Billing Statement
   - Legal Correspondence
   - Employer Report
   - Investigation Report
   - Utilization Review
   - AME/QME Report
   - Deposition Transcript
   - Imaging Report
   - Pharmacy Record
   - Wage Statement
   - Benefit Notice
   - Settlement Document
   - Correspondence
   - Other

   The classification includes a confidence score so you can verify the AI's assessment.

3. **Field Extraction** — For structured documents (like DWC-1 forms, medical reports, and wage statements), the system extracts key data fields automatically. Each extracted field includes a confidence score and a reference to the source page.

4. **Investigation Checklist Auto-Completion** — When certain document types are uploaded, the corresponding investigation checklist item is automatically marked complete. For example:
   - Uploading a DWC-1 Claim Form automatically completes the "DWC-1 Claim Form on File" checklist item
   - Uploading a Medical Report automatically completes the "Medical Records Requested" item
   - Uploading an Employer Report automatically completes the "Employer Report Received" item
   - Uploading a Wage Statement automatically completes the "Average Weekly Earnings Verified" item

5. **Embedding and Indexing** — The document text is split into chunks and converted into vector embeddings. These embeddings power the AI chat's ability to search across your claim documents and cite specific passages when answering your questions.

### Document Access Levels

AdjudiCLAIMS uses three access levels for documents:

- **EXAMINER_ONLY** — Visible to claims examiners, supervisors, and administrators. This is the default for most documents.
- **SHARED** — Visible to all authorized users of the claim, including in future integrations with the attorney product.
- **ATTORNEY_ONLY** — Restricted to attorney users only. Claims examiners cannot view these documents. This protects attorney work product and privileged communications.

Additionally, documents are flagged if they contain:
- **Legal analysis** — Content that includes legal reasoning or conclusions
- **Work product** — Attorney work product protected by privilege
- **Privileged material** — Attorney-client privileged communications

These flags help ensure that privileged and work-product materials are not inadvertently shared with non-attorney users.

### Document Timeline

As documents are uploaded and processed, AdjudiCLAIMS can generate timeline events from the documents. For example, a medical report dated January 15 that describes a surgery on January 10 creates a timeline entry for the surgery date. These timeline events build a chronological picture of the claim that you can review alongside the deadline tracking and investigation checklist.

The document timeline is particularly useful during the coverage determination process, when you need to understand the sequence of events (injury, treatment, diagnostic findings, work status changes) in chronological order.

### Best Practices for Document Management

- Upload all documents as soon as you receive them, even if you have not reviewed them yet. The system will classify and index them for you.
- Review the AI's document classification. If a document is misclassified, the classification confidence score will usually be lower, signaling that manual review is appropriate.
- Pay attention to the auto-completion of investigation items when you upload documents. If a document type maps to a checklist item, saving you a manual step, verify that the auto-completion is appropriate for your claim.

## 5. AI Chat — Your Research Assistant

The AI chat is your primary tool for asking questions about a claim. It searches through all of the documents in the claim file, retrieves relevant information, and provides cited answers.

### How to Use the AI Chat

1. Open the claim you want to ask about.
2. Navigate to the Chat section.
3. Type your question in plain English. For example:
   - "What is the claimant's date of injury?"
   - "What body parts were diagnosed by the QME?"
   - "What is the claimant's AWE based on the wage statement?"
   - "When is the next TD payment due?"
   - "Summarize the medical records in this file."
4. The AI will search the claim documents, retrieve relevant passages, and provide an answer with citations.

Each response shows you:
- The answer to your question
- The UPL zone classification (GREEN, YELLOW, or RED)
- Citations to the specific documents and passages the answer is based on
- A disclaimer if the response is in the YELLOW zone

### The Three Zones: GREEN, YELLOW, and RED

Every question you ask is classified into one of three zones before the AI generates a response. This classification is the most important compliance feature in AdjudiCLAIMS.

#### GREEN Zone — Factual Information (Safe)

GREEN zone questions ask for factual data, arithmetic calculations, document citations, or procedural information. The AI answers these directly.

Examples of GREEN zone questions:
- "What did the QME diagnose?"
- "What is the TD rate for an AWE of $1,200 with a 2025 injury date?"
- "When was the DWC-1 received?"
- "List all medical reports in this file."
- "What body parts are listed on the DWC-1?"
- "What is the 15-day acknowledgment deadline for this claim?"

GREEN zone responses include full citations to source documents. No disclaimer is needed because the information is purely factual.

**Scenario:** You ask the AI chat: "What is the claimant's AWE based on the wage statement?" The system classifies this as GREEN (factual data request), searches the claim documents, finds the wage statement, and responds: "Based on the wage statement from Acme Manufacturing dated February 20, 2026, the claimant's gross earnings for the 52 weeks prior to injury totaled $78,000.00. The Average Weekly Earnings (AWE) is $78,000.00 / 52 = $1,500.00 per week. Source: Wage Statement, page 1."

#### YELLOW Zone — Statistical Data with Disclaimer

YELLOW zone questions involve statistical or comparative information that is factual but could be misinterpreted as legal advice without proper context. The AI answers these but always includes a mandatory disclaimer.

Examples of YELLOW zone questions:
- "What is the typical settlement range for lumbar spine claims in this jurisdiction?"
- "How do comparable claims usually resolve?"
- "What is the average duration of TD for this type of injury?"

YELLOW zone responses always include a disclaimer such as: "This information is statistical and provided for reference only. Each claim is unique. Consult defense counsel for case-specific guidance."

**Scenario:** You ask: "What is the typical duration of TD for a lumbar disc herniation?" The system classifies this as YELLOW (statistical data), and responds with aggregate data: "Based on comparable claims data, lumbar disc herniation claims typically involve 12 to 26 weeks of temporary disability, depending on whether surgery is involved. Non-surgical cases average 12-16 weeks; surgical cases average 20-26 weeks." The response includes the mandatory disclaimer: "This information is statistical and provided for reference only. Each claim is unique. Consult defense counsel for case-specific guidance."

#### RED Zone — Blocked (Legal Territory)

RED zone questions ask for legal advice, legal analysis, case evaluation, or strategic recommendations. The AI will not answer these questions. Instead, it will explain why the question requires legal analysis and offer to help you prepare a factual summary to send to your defense counsel.

Examples of RED zone questions:
- "Should I accept or deny this claim?"
- "What is this claim worth?"
- "Is coverage clear in this case?"
- "Do we have a strong defense?"
- "Should we settle?"
- "What legal strategy should we use?"

When your question is classified as RED, you will see a message like: "This question requires legal analysis that falls outside the scope of claims examination. Contact your assigned defense counsel. Would you like me to prepare a factual claim summary for your attorney?"

**Scenario:** You ask: "Should I accept or deny this claim based on the medical evidence?" The system classifies this as RED (requests a recommendation on a substantive claim decision) and responds: "This question asks for a recommendation on whether to accept or deny the claim. That is a legal determination that requires analysis of compensability (AOE/COE) by your defense counsel. I can help you by preparing a factual summary of the medical evidence, claim data, and deadlines to send to your attorney. Would you like me to generate a counsel referral summary?"

Note the difference: "What did the QME diagnose?" is GREEN (asking for a fact from a document), while "Should I accept this claim?" is RED (asking for a legal judgment). The key distinction is between asking the AI to retrieve facts versus asking the AI to make decisions or recommendations.

### Chat Sessions

Each conversation with the AI is organized into sessions. A chat session is tied to a specific claim and a specific user. You can have multiple sessions for the same claim (for example, one session for medical record questions and another for benefit calculation questions). Sessions preserve your conversation history so you can refer back to previous questions and answers.

Supervisors and administrators can view all chat sessions for claims in their organization. Examiners can only see their own sessions.

**Why does AdjudiCLAIMS block these questions?** Under California law (Business and Professions Code section 6125), providing legal advice is the practice of law. Only licensed attorneys may practice law. AdjudiCLAIMS is designed for claims examiners, not attorneys. By blocking legal questions and directing them to counsel, the system protects you and your organization from unauthorized practice of law issues.

### Understanding "Consult Defense Counsel" Messages

When you see a message directing you to consult defense counsel, it means your question involves legal judgment that only a licensed attorney should provide. This is not a limitation of the AI — it is a legal requirement.

AdjudiCLAIMS helps bridge this gap by offering to generate a factual counsel referral summary (see Section 16) that you can send to your attorney. The summary contains all the factual data from the claim file, organized into sections your attorney needs, without any legal analysis.

### Citation Verification

Every response from the AI chat includes citations. A citation tells you:
- Which document the information came from
- A snippet of the relevant text from that document

You should always verify important facts by reviewing the cited source document. AdjudiCLAIMS provides the citations so you can trace every statement back to its source. This is the Glass Box principle in action: nothing is hidden, and you can always check the AI's work.

## 6. Regulatory Deadline Tracking

AdjudiCLAIMS automatically calculates and tracks every regulatory deadline for your claims. Deadlines are generated when a claim is created and are based on the date the claim was received.

### Deadline Types

The system tracks the following California Workers' Compensation deadlines:

| Deadline | Time Frame | Statutory Authority | What It Means |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| 15-Day Acknowledgment | 15 calendar days from receipt | 10 CCR 2695.5(b) | You must send a written acknowledgment to the injured worker within 15 days of receiving the claim |
| 40-Day Determination | 40 calendar days from receipt | 10 CCR 2695.7(b) | You must accept or deny the claim within 40 days (or issue a delay notice explaining why you need more time) |
| 14-Day First TD Payment | 14 calendar days from employer knowledge | LC 4650 | If the claim involves lost wages, the first Temporary Disability payment must be issued within 14 days |
| 14-Day Subsequent TD Payment | Every 14 calendar days | LC 4650 | After the first payment, TD must be paid every 14 days until TD ends |
| 30-Day Delay Notice | 30 calendar days from receipt | 10 CCR 2695.7(c) | If you cannot determine compensability within 40 days, you must send a delay notice to the claimant |
| 5-Day Prospective UR | 5 business days | LC 4610 | Prospective utilization review decisions must be made within 5 business days |
| 30-Day Retrospective UR | 30 business days | LC 4610 | Retrospective utilization review decisions must be made within 30 business days |
| 15-Day Employer Notification | 15 calendar days | LC 3761 | You must notify the employer of their rights and obligations within 15 days |

Note: Utilization review deadlines are calculated in business days, which exclude weekends and California state holidays. All other deadlines use calendar days.

### Urgency Levels

Each deadline is color-coded based on how much time has elapsed:

- **GREEN** — Less than 50% of the allowed time has passed. You have plenty of time.
- **YELLOW** — Between 50% and 80% of the time has passed. Start working on this soon.
- **RED** — More than 80% of the time has passed but the deadline has not yet arrived. Act immediately.
- **OVERDUE** — The deadline has passed. If the action was not completed, this is a compliance violation.

Your dashboard sorts deadlines by urgency, with OVERDUE and RED deadlines appearing first.

**Practical example:** You created a claim on March 1 with a 15-day acknowledgment deadline due March 16. On March 5, the deadline is GREEN (33% elapsed, 11 days remaining). On March 10, it turns YELLOW (60% elapsed, 6 days remaining). On March 14, it turns RED (87% elapsed, 2 days remaining). If you have not sent the acknowledgment by March 16, the deadline becomes OVERDUE. At that point, you can still mark it as met if you send the acknowledgment, but the system records that it was completed after the due date, which counts as a missed deadline for compliance reporting.

### California State Holidays

Business day calculations (used for utilization review deadlines) exclude weekends and California state holidays. The system automatically accounts for the following holidays:
- New Year's Day (January 1)
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day (3rd Monday of January)
- Presidents' Day (3rd Monday of February)
- Cesar Chavez Day (March 31)
- Memorial Day (last Monday of May)
- Juneteenth (June 19)
- Independence Day (July 4)
- Labor Day (1st Monday of September)
- Indigenous Peoples' Day (2nd Monday of October)
- Veterans Day (November 11)
- Thanksgiving (4th Thursday of November)
- Day after Thanksgiving
- Christmas Day (December 25)

When a fixed-date holiday falls on a Saturday, it is observed on Friday. When it falls on a Sunday, it is observed on Monday.

### How to Mark Deadlines as Met

When you complete the required action for a deadline (for example, sending the acknowledgment letter):

1. Open the claim.
2. Navigate to the Deadlines section.
3. Find the deadline you have satisfied.
4. Mark it as "Met."

The system records the completion date and time. If the action was completed after the due date, the deadline is marked as missed rather than met.

Supervisors can also mark deadlines as "Waived" in exceptional circumstances, with a required explanation.

### Deadline Summary

For each claim, you can view a summary showing:
- Total deadlines
- How many are pending, met, missed, or waived
- How many are in the urgent category (RED or OVERDUE)

This gives you a quick compliance snapshot for each claim.

## 7. Benefit Calculator

The benefit calculator performs statutory arithmetic for California Workers' Compensation benefits. All calculations follow the published formulas in the California Labor Code. This is a GREEN zone feature — it is pure math, not legal advice.

### TD Rate Calculation

Temporary Disability (TD) benefits compensate an injured worker for lost wages during recovery. The TD rate is calculated as follows:

**Formula:** TD Rate = 2/3 of the Average Weekly Earnings (AWE), subject to the statutory minimum and maximum for the injury year.

**Statutory authority:** Labor Code section 4653

To calculate a TD rate:

1. Open the claim.
2. Navigate to the Benefit Calculator.
3. Enter the Average Weekly Earnings (AWE). This should come from the verified wage statement.
4. The system uses the date of injury to determine which year's statutory minimum and maximum rates apply.
5. The calculator shows you:
   - The AWE you entered
   - The calculated TD rate (2/3 of AWE)
   - Whether the rate was adjusted to the statutory minimum (if the calculated rate was below the floor) or the statutory maximum (if it was above the cap)
   - The applicable statutory minimum and maximum for the injury year
   - The statutory authority (LC 4653)

For reference, the statutory TD rate ranges are:

| Injury Year | Minimum Weekly Rate | Maximum Weekly Rate |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 2024 | $230.95 | $1,619.15 |
| 2025 | $242.86 | $1,694.57 |
| 2026 | $252.43 | $1,761.71 |

**Example:** For a 2025 injury with an AWE of $1,500:
- TD Rate = $1,500 x 2/3 = $1,000.00 per week
- This falls between the 2025 minimum ($242.86) and maximum ($1,694.57), so no adjustment is needed.

**Example:** For a 2025 injury with an AWE of $3,000:
- TD Rate = $3,000 x 2/3 = $2,000.00 per week
- This exceeds the 2025 maximum of $1,694.57, so the rate is capped at $1,694.57 per week.

Every calculation includes a disclaimer: "This benefit calculation applies the statutory formula to the data provided. It is arithmetic only. Verify inputs against source documents."

**Example with minimum clamping:** For a 2025 injury with an AWE of $300:
- TD Rate = $300 x 2/3 = $200.00 per week
- This is below the 2025 minimum of $242.86, so the rate is raised to the statutory minimum of $242.86 per week. The system notes that the rate was clamped to the minimum.

### Payment Schedules

The calculator can generate a full payment schedule showing:

- Payment number
- Due date (every 14 calendar days per LC 4650)
- Payment amount (biweekly = 2 weeks of the weekly TD rate)
- Period covered (start and end dates)
- Whether the payment was late (if you provide actual payment dates)
- Penalty amount for late payments (10% per LC 4650(c))

The first payment is due 14 calendar days after the employer's date of knowledge of the injury. Subsequent payments are due every 14 calendar days after that.

If the TD period ends mid-cycle, the final payment is prorated based on the actual number of days in the partial period.

**Practical example:** An injured worker has a TD rate of $1,000.00 per week. The employer's date of knowledge is March 1, 2026, and TD ends on April 30, 2026.

- Payment 1: Due March 15, covering March 1-14. Amount: $2,000.00 (2 weeks x $1,000.00)
- Payment 2: Due March 29, covering March 15-28. Amount: $2,000.00
- Payment 3: Due April 12, covering March 29-April 11. Amount: $2,000.00
- Payment 4: Due April 26, covering April 12-25. Amount: $2,000.00
- Payment 5: Due May 10, covering April 26-30 (5 days of a 14-day period). Amount: $714.29 (5/14 x $2,000.00)

Total TD: $8,714.29 over 61 days.

### Late Payment Penalties

Under Labor Code section 4650(c), if a TD payment is late, the employer must self-impose a 10% penalty on the late payment. This is not optional — it is a statutory requirement.

AdjudiCLAIMS calculates the penalty automatically when you provide actual payment dates that fall after the due dates. The penalty is 10% of the late payment amount.

**Example:** A biweekly TD payment of $2,000.00 was due on March 15 but was not paid until March 22. The penalty is $2,000.00 x 10% = $200.00. The total payment due is $2,200.00.

### Death Benefit Calculations

For fatal claims, the calculator determines death benefits under Labor Code sections 4700 through 4706:

- **Total dependents** receive the full statutory death benefit amount for the injury year.
- **Partial dependents** receive a proportional share based on the degree of dependency.

Death benefit payments are made at the maximum TD rate for the injury year until the total statutory amount is exhausted.

| Injury Year | Total Death Benefit |
|------------|-------------------|
| 2024 | $290,000 |
| 2025 | $310,000 |
| 2026 | $320,000 |

The calculator shows the total benefit, the weekly payment rate, and the expected number of weeks of payments.

## 8. Investigation Checklist

When a new claim is created, AdjudiCLAIMS automatically generates a 10-item investigation checklist. This checklist reflects the standard investigation requirements for California Workers' Compensation claims.

### The Ten Investigation Items

1. **Three-Point Contact: Injured Worker** — Initial contact with the injured worker to gather claim details. This is one of the three mandatory contacts you must make at the outset of every claim.

2. **Three-Point Contact: Employer** — Contact the employer to verify employment and injury circumstances. The employer can confirm dates, job duties, and whether the injury was witnessed.

3. **Three-Point Contact: Medical Provider** — Contact the treating physician for medical status and work restrictions. You need to know the diagnosis, treatment plan, and any work limitations.

4. **Recorded Statement** — Obtain a recorded statement from the injured worker. This documents the worker's account of how the injury occurred.

5. **Employer Report Received** — The employer's report of occupational injury or illness must be on file. This is automatically marked complete when you upload an Employer Report document.

6. **Medical Records Requested** — Request and obtain treating physician medical records. This is automatically marked complete when you upload a Medical Report document.

7. **DWC-1 Claim Form on File** — The Workers' Compensation claim form (DWC-1) must be received and filed. This is automatically marked complete when you upload a DWC-1 Claim Form document.

8. **Index Bureau / Prior Claims Search** — Search for prior Workers' Compensation claims by this applicant. Prior claims may affect your investigation.

9. **Average Weekly Earnings Verified** — Verify the AWE from wage statements for benefit calculation. This is automatically marked complete when you upload a Wage Statement document.

10. **Initial Reserves Established** — Set initial indemnity, medical, legal, and lien reserves. Reserves represent your estimate of the total future cost of the claim.

### How Items Are Completed

Items can be completed in two ways:

**Automatic completion:** When you upload certain document types, the corresponding checklist item is automatically marked complete. A document icon appears next to automatically completed items, linking to the document that satisfied the requirement.

**Manual completion:** For items that are not tied to document uploads (such as three-point contacts, recorded statements, and index bureau checks), you mark them complete manually. Open the claim, navigate to the Investigation section, and check off the item.

### Progress Tracking

The investigation checklist shows your progress at a glance:
- Number of items completed out of 10
- Percentage complete
- Which items are still outstanding

Supervisors can view investigation progress across all claims in the organization, helping identify claims where investigation is lagging.

### Undoing Completion

Supervisors and administrators can undo the completion of a checklist item if it was marked complete in error. This resets the item to incomplete and clears the completion record.

### Investigation and Claim Status

Investigation progress is closely tied to the claim's lifecycle. A typical flow looks like this:

1. Claim is created with status OPEN. The investigation checklist is generated.
2. You begin your investigation (three-point contacts, gathering documents). You change the status to UNDER INVESTIGATION.
3. As you gather documents, investigation items are auto-completed. You manually complete items like three-point contacts and recorded statements.
4. When the investigation is substantially complete, you use the Coverage Determination workflow to decide whether to accept, delay, or deny the claim.
5. You change the status to ACCEPTED or DENIED and issue the appropriate letter.

The investigation checklist completion percentage is one of the factors in your organization's DOI Audit Readiness Score. Claims with incomplete investigations represent a compliance risk.

## 10. Lien Management and OMFS Comparison

A lien is a claim filed by a third party (usually a medical provider) seeking payment from the workers' compensation claim. Lien management is a critical part of claims administration.

### Creating and Tracking Liens

To record a new lien on a claim:

1. Open the claim.
2. Navigate to the Liens section.
3. Select "New Lien."
4. Enter the lien details:
   - **Lien claimant** — Who filed the lien (for example, the name of the medical provider)
   - **Lien type** — The category of lien:
     - Medical Provider — A healthcare provider seeking payment for treatment
     - Attorney Fee — An attorney claiming fees
     - EDD — Employment Development Department seeking reimbursement
     - Expense — Expense reimbursement
     - Child Support — Child support lien
     - Other — Any other type of lien
   - **Total amount claimed** — The dollar amount the lien claimant is seeking
   - **Filing date** — When the lien was filed
   - **Filing fee status** — Whether the filing fee has been paid (required under LC 4903.1 for liens filed after January 1, 2013)
   - **WCAB case number** — If applicable, the Workers' Compensation Appeals Board case number
   - **Notes** — Any relevant notes

### Lien Status Lifecycle

Each lien moves through a defined lifecycle:

1. **RECEIVED** — The lien has been received and logged.
2. **UNDER REVIEW** — You are reviewing the lien and its supporting documentation.
3. **OMFS COMPARED** — The lien's billed amounts have been compared against the Official Medical Fee Schedule. The system can transition to this status automatically when you run the OMFS comparison.
4. **NEGOTIATING** — You are negotiating the lien amount with the lien claimant.
5. **PAID IN FULL** — The lien has been paid at the full amount claimed.
6. **PAID REDUCED** — The lien has been paid at a reduced (negotiated) amount.
7. **DISPUTED** — The lien is disputed and may be heading to a hearing.
8. **WCAB HEARING** — The lien dispute is being heard by the WCAB.
9. **RESOLVED BY ORDER** — The WCAB has issued an order resolving the lien.
10. **WITHDRAWN** — The lien claimant has withdrawn the lien.

Not all transitions are allowed. For example, a lien that is RECEIVED can move to UNDER REVIEW or WITHDRAWN, but not directly to PAID IN FULL. The system enforces these transition rules to maintain data integrity.

### OMFS Bill Comparison

The Official Medical Fee Schedule (OMFS) sets the maximum reimbursement rates for medical services in California Workers' Compensation (8 CCR 9789.10 et seq.). AdjudiCLAIMS can compare a lien's billed amounts against the OMFS to identify overcharges.

To run an OMFS comparison:

1. Open the lien.
2. Add line items for each service billed. Each line item needs:
   - Service date
   - CPT code (the medical procedure code)
   - Description
   - Amount claimed
3. Run the OMFS comparison.
4. The system will look up the OMFS rate for each CPT code and show you:
   - The billed amount vs. the OMFS-allowed amount for each line item
   - Whether the line item is an overcharge
   - The overcharge amount for each line item
   - The total amount claimed, total OMFS-allowed amount, and total discrepancy

This is a factual comparison (GREEN zone). AdjudiCLAIMS tells you the numbers. It does not tell you whether to pay, negotiate, or dispute the lien — that may involve legal judgment and should be discussed with your supervisor or defense counsel when necessary.

**Practical example:** A medical provider lien claims $15,000 for treatment of the injured worker's lumbar spine. You add the line items:

| Service Date | CPT Code | Description | Amount Claimed | OMFS Rate | Overcharge? |
|-------------|----------|-------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| 2026-01-15 | 72148 | MRI lumbar spine | $3,500.00 | $1,200.00 | Yes — $2,300.00 |
| 2026-02-01 | 97110 | Therapeutic exercises (12 units) | $2,400.00 | $1,800.00 | Yes — $600.00 |
| 2026-02-15 | 62322 | Lumbar epidural injection | $9,100.00 | $3,500.00 | Yes — $5,600.00 |

The OMFS comparison shows:
- Total claimed: $15,000.00
- Total OMFS allowed: $6,500.00
- Total discrepancy: $8,500.00

This factual data helps you understand the scope of the overcharge before discussing next steps with your supervisor.

### Filing Compliance Check

AdjudiCLAIMS can check whether a lien meets the filing requirements under LC 4903.1:

- Has the filing fee been paid? (Required for liens filed after January 1, 2013)
- Is the lien claimant identified?
- Is the filing date present?
- For medical provider liens, are itemized line items present?

If any of these checks fail, the system will list the specific issues. This is a factual compliance check — it tells you whether the paperwork is in order, not whether the lien itself is legally valid.

### Lien Exposure

The Lien Summary shows the total lien exposure for a claim:
- Total number of liens
- Total amount claimed across all liens
- Total OMFS-allowed amount (for liens that have been compared)
- Total discrepancy between claimed and allowed amounts
- Number of active (unresolved) liens
- Breakdown by status and by type

This gives you a quick picture of the lien burden on the claim.

### When to Involve Defense Counsel

Lien tracking and OMFS comparison are factual operations that you perform as a claims examiner. However, certain lien situations require legal judgment:

- Evaluating whether a lien is legally valid
- Developing a settlement strategy for multiple liens
- Representing the employer at a WCAB lien hearing
- Determining whether to contest a lien based on legal arguments

When you encounter these situations, use the Counsel Referral feature (Section 16) to prepare a factual summary for your defense attorney.

## 11. MTUS Guideline Matching

MTUS stands for the Medical Treatment Utilization Schedule. These are evidence-based treatment guidelines adopted by the Division of Workers' Compensation (DWC) based on the American College of Occupational and Environmental Medicine (ACOEM) guidelines. MTUS guidelines establish the standard of care for medical treatment in California Workers' Compensation.

### What MTUS Matching Does

When a treatment request comes in for utilization review, you can use MTUS matching to look up the relevant guideline criteria for the requested treatment. The system matches the treatment request against the MTUS guidelines based on:

- The injured body part
- The diagnosis (if available)
- The treatment being requested
- The CPT code (if available)

### How to Use MTUS Matching

1. Open the claim.
2. Navigate to the MTUS section.
3. Enter the treatment request details:
   - **Body part** — The body part being treated (for example, "lumbar spine," "knee," "shoulder")
   - **Diagnosis** — The medical diagnosis, if known
   - **Treatment description** — What treatment is being requested
   - **CPT code** — The procedure code, if available
4. Run the match.

The system returns matching guidelines, each showing:
- The guideline title and source section
- The guideline text describing what the medical evidence supports
- Recommended frequency and duration (if applicable)
- The evidence level (for example, "Evidence Level A -- Strong" or "Evidence Level B -- Moderate")
- A relevance score indicating how closely the guideline matches your query

### Understanding the Results

MTUS matching results are factual. They show you what the published medical guidelines say about a particular treatment for a particular body part. The results include guidelines for common body parts including the lumbar spine, cervical spine, shoulder, knee, and wrist.

**Important:** AdjudiCLAIMS presents guideline criteria. It does not make treatment recommendations. The clinical decision about whether to authorize, modify, or deny a treatment request must be made by the Utilization Review physician reviewer per Labor Code section 4610.

Every MTUS match result includes this disclaimer: "MTUS guideline matching is provided for utilization review reference only. Clinical decisions regarding treatment authorization must be made by the UR physician reviewer per LC 4610. AdjudiCLAIMS presents guideline criteria -- it does not make treatment recommendations."

### Using Results for Utilization Review Support

As a claims examiner, you use the MTUS matching results to:

- Understand what the evidence-based guidelines say about the requested treatment
- Provide relevant guideline information to the UR physician reviewer
- Track whether treatment requests align with MTUS criteria
- Document the guideline basis for UR decisions in the claim file

You do not make the medical decision yourself. You gather the guideline information and provide it to the qualified UR reviewer.

**Practical example:** A treating physician requests authorization for an MRI of the lumbar spine for a worker who has had back pain for 3 weeks with no red flags. You run an MTUS match with body part "lumbar spine" and treatment description "MRI lumbar spine."

The system returns a match to the MTUS guideline on lumbar spine imaging (Evidence Level A — Strong), which states: "Imaging is not recommended for acute low back pain in the absence of red flags. MRI is recommended when radiculopathy persists beyond 6 weeks despite conservative treatment, or when surgical intervention is being considered."

You provide this guideline information to the UR physician reviewer, who uses it along with the complete medical record to make the clinical authorization decision. You document the guideline match in the claim file.

## 14. Education System

AdjudiCLAIMS is designed to be your ongoing training resource, not just a claims management tool. The education system has three layers.

### Tier 1: Dismissable Term Definitions

When you encounter a regulatory term for the first time (for example, "AWE" or "TD" or "AOE/COE"), AdjudiCLAIMS shows you a brief definition and explanation.

- AWE stands for Average Weekly Earnings — the average weekly wages earned by the injured worker, used to calculate benefit rates.
- TD stands for Temporary Disability — benefits paid to compensate an injured worker for lost wages during recovery.
- AOE/COE stands for Arising Out of Employment / Course of Employment — the two conditions that must be met for an injury to be compensable under workers' compensation.

Once you understand a term, you can dismiss it. The system remembers your dismissals, and you will not see that definition again. This lets you learn at your own pace without being overwhelmed by information you already know.

New examiners in their first 30 days have a "learning mode" that provides extended tooltips and additional guidance across the system. After 30 days, this extra guidance is reduced, but you can always look up any term in the education section.

### Tier 2: Always-Present Regulatory Education

Tier 2 education is never hidden and never dismissable. These are the core regulatory explanations that accompany every feature in AdjudiCLAIMS:

- When you see a deadline, you see the statutory authority and an explanation of why the deadline exists and what happens if it is missed.
- When you use the benefit calculator, you see the formula, the statutory citation, and the consequences of incorrect calculations.
- When you work through a decision workflow, every step explains not just what to do but why the regulation requires it.

This is the Glass Box principle: the regulatory context is always visible. Even experienced examiners benefit from seeing the "why" behind every requirement, because regulations change and memory is imperfect.

### Layer 3: Ongoing Education

Beyond initial training, AdjudiCLAIMS provides four channels of continuous education:

1. **Regulatory Change Notifications** — When California Workers' Compensation laws or regulations change, the system notifies you. You must acknowledge each change, confirming that you have read and understood the update. Your acknowledgments are tracked in your education profile.

2. **Monthly Compliance Reviews** — Each month, the system generates a personalized review for you showing:
   - Deadlines you missed in the past month
   - Deadlines approaching in the next 30 days
   - Claims with no recent activity (stale claims that may need attention)

   This helps you stay on top of your caseload and identify patterns in your work.

3. **Quarterly Refreshers** — Every quarter, you complete a brief assessment covering key regulatory requirements. This keeps your knowledge current. The refresher includes questions on recent regulatory changes and core compliance concepts. Your scores and completion dates are tracked.

4. **Audit-Triggered Training** — If a DOI audit identifies specific compliance findings, your supervisor can assign targeted training modules to address those findings. Completion of audit-triggered training is tracked separately and includes a score requirement.

Your education profile tracks all of this: dismissed terms, completed training modules, acknowledged regulatory changes, monthly review completions, quarterly refresher scores, audit-triggered training completions, and your last annual recertification date.

### Learning Mode for New Examiners

When you first join the system, you enter a 30-day learning mode. During this period:

- Extended tooltips and guidance appear throughout the interface
- Tier 1 term definitions are shown more prominently
- Workflow steps include additional context and examples

After 30 days, the learning mode expires and the interface adjusts to the standard experience. You can still access all educational content at any time through the education section — learning mode simply makes it more prominent during your first month.

### How Education Supports Compliance

The education system serves a dual purpose:

1. **For you:** It helps you learn and stay current on California Workers' Compensation regulations. Even experienced examiners encounter regulatory changes, and the quarterly refreshers help maintain proficiency.

2. **For your organization:** Education completion is tracked and reported. Training completion rates appear on the supervisor and admin compliance dashboards. During a DOI audit, your organization can demonstrate that every examiner has been trained, has acknowledged regulatory changes, and has completed ongoing education requirements.

## 16. Counsel Referral System

When AdjudiCLAIMS identifies that your question requires legal analysis (a RED zone classification), or when you independently determine that a legal issue needs attorney involvement, the counsel referral system helps you communicate effectively with your defense counsel.

### When the System Directs You to Counsel

You will be directed to counsel when:
- You ask an AI chat question that is classified as RED zone (legal advice, case valuation, strategy)
- A decision workflow step is flagged as YELLOW zone, indicating legal complexity
- You encounter a situation that requires legal judgment (coverage disputes, denial basis, settlement authority, lien validity)

### How to Create a Counsel Referral

1. When you receive a RED zone message in the AI chat, the system will offer to prepare a factual summary for your attorney.
2. Describe the legal issue in plain language. For example: "Need counsel's opinion on whether this cumulative trauma claim has an AOE/COE basis" or "Applicant attorney is disputing the denial — need legal review."
3. The system generates a factual summary with six sections:
   - **Claim Overview** — Basic claim information (claim number, claimant, date of injury, employer, body parts, status)
   - **Medical Evidence Summary** — A summary of the medical documentation on file
   - **Benefits Status** — Financial information (amounts paid, reserves)
   - **Claim Timeline** — Key dates (injury, received, acknowledged, determined)
   - **Legal Issue Identified** — The legal issue you described
   - **Documents Available** — A list of documents in the claim file (excluding privileged and work-product materials)
4. Review the summary before sending it to your attorney.

The summary contains only factual information. It never includes legal analysis, opinions, or recommendations — those are for your attorney to provide.

### How Referrals Are Tracked

Each counsel referral is tracked with a status:

- **PENDING** — The referral has been created but not yet sent to counsel
- **SENT** — The referral has been sent to the defense attorney
- **RESPONDED** — Defense counsel has provided a response
- **CLOSED** — The referral has been resolved

You can update the referral status as it progresses, add the counsel's email address, and record their response when it comes back. All status changes are logged in the audit trail.

### Providing Information to Defense Counsel

The counsel referral summary is designed to give your attorney everything they need to analyze the legal issue without calling you for basic claim facts. It includes:

- All claim identification and financial data
- The complete deadline status
- A list of all documents on file (with type and date, but not the document content itself — your attorney can request specific documents through normal channels)
- The specific legal issue you need addressed

This saves time for both you and your attorney by providing a structured, complete handoff.

**Practical example:** You are handling a cumulative trauma claim where the applicant's attorney is arguing that the date of injury should be different from what you determined. This is a legal question — the determination of the date of injury in a cumulative trauma case involves legal analysis.

You create a counsel referral:
1. Legal issue: "Applicant attorney disputes date of injury for cumulative trauma claim. Attorney argues DOI should be 2025-06-15 based on last day worked. We used 2025-09-01 based on date of disability per LC 5412. Need legal analysis of correct DOI."
2. AdjudiCLAIMS generates a summary with all claim data, medical evidence, financial status, timeline, the legal issue description, and available documents.
3. You mark the referral as SENT after forwarding it to your defense attorney.
4. When counsel responds with their analysis, you update the referral to RESPONDED and record their guidance.
5. After acting on counsel's advice, you close the referral.

The entire referral lifecycle is tracked in the audit trail.

## 17. Understanding UPL Compliance

UPL stands for the Unauthorized Practice of Law. Understanding why AdjudiCLAIMS operates the way it does will help you use the system more effectively.

### The Legal Background

Under California Business and Professions Code section 6125, the practice of law is restricted to licensed attorneys. The practice of law includes:

- Providing legal advice
- Interpreting the law as applied to specific facts
- Making legal recommendations
- Evaluating the legal strength of a position
- Advising on legal strategy

Claims examiners perform essential work that involves applying regulations to factual situations. But there is a line between factual/administrative work (which examiners do) and legal analysis (which only attorneys may do). AdjudiCLAIMS is designed to stay firmly on the examiner's side of that line.

### The Examiner's Role vs. the Attorney's Role

**What examiners do (and what AdjudiCLAIMS supports):**
- Gather factual information about a claim
- Calculate benefits using statutory formulas
- Track and meet regulatory deadlines
- Investigate claims (three-point contact, recorded statements, medical records)
- Process documents and maintain the claim file
- Follow established procedures and workflows
- Identify when a legal issue exists and refer it to counsel

**What attorneys do (and what AdjudiCLAIMS does not do):**
- Analyze whether specific facts satisfy legal standards
- Advise on whether to accept, deny, or settle a claim based on legal considerations
- Evaluate the strength of a legal position
- Develop litigation strategy
- Provide legal conclusions about coverage, liability, or valuation
- Draft legal pleadings and make legal arguments before the WCAB

### How the System Protects You

AdjudiCLAIMS has three layers of UPL protection:

1. **Query Classification (Pre-Chat)** — Before the AI generates a response, your question is analyzed by a two-stage classifier. The first stage uses keyword patterns to identify clearly legal questions (fast, less than a millisecond). The second stage uses AI classification for nuanced questions (about half a second). If there is any doubt, the system defaults to RED (blocked). This conservative approach means the system may occasionally block a question that an examiner could legitimately ask, but it will never answer a question it should not.

2. **System Prompt Enforcement (During Chat)** — The AI's instructions (called "system prompts") explicitly define the examiner's role and prohibit legal analysis. The AI is told that it serves claims examiners who are not attorneys, and that it must never provide legal advice, case evaluations, or strategic recommendations.

3. **Output Validation (Post-Chat)** — After the AI generates a response, the output is scanned for prohibited language patterns. Even if a response somehow gets past the query classifier and system prompt, the output validator catches it. Phrases like "you should deny," "the claim is worth," "I recommend settling," or "liability is likely" are flagged and blocked before you see them.

These three layers work together to create a robust compliance boundary. Every classification, block, and disclaimer is logged in the audit trail, providing a complete record for compliance review.

### What This Means for You

- **Do not worry about asking the "wrong" question.** If your question crosses the line, AdjudiCLAIMS will tell you and offer to help you connect with counsel. You are not penalized for asking RED zone questions.
- **Trust the redirections.** When the system tells you to consult counsel, it is protecting you and your organization. Use the counsel referral feature to make that consultation efficient.
- **Verify the facts.** AdjudiCLAIMS provides cited factual information. Always check the citations, especially for high-stakes decisions.
- **The system gets smarter over time.** The classification and validation rules are continuously refined to reduce false positives (legitimate questions incorrectly blocked) while maintaining full protection against legal advice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I asked a factual question but it was blocked as RED zone. What do I do?**
A: The system errs on the side of caution. If you believe your question is factual, try rephrasing it to focus on specific data rather than conclusions. For example, instead of "Is this a compensable injury?" (RED — asks for a legal conclusion), try "What are the documented facts about how the injury occurred?" (GREEN — asks for factual data). If the system consistently blocks questions you believe are appropriate, report this to your supervisor so the classification rules can be reviewed.

**Q: Why do I have to complete training before I can use the system?**
A: California regulation 10 CCR 2695.6 requires insurers to maintain training standards for claims professionals. The four training modules ensure that every examiner understands the regulatory framework, the UPL compliance system, deadline requirements, and the tools available. This training also protects you personally — it demonstrates that you were trained on the system's capabilities and limitations.

**Q: Can I undo a deadline that I accidentally marked as met?**
A: Examiners cannot undo deadline completions. Contact your supervisor, who has the ability to review and adjust deadline statuses when necessary.

**Q: What happens if I upload the wrong document to a claim?**
A: Contact your supervisor. Document deletions are restricted operations that require supervisor or administrator access. The deletion will be logged in the audit trail.

**Q: How do I know if a letter I generated was actually sent?**
A: AdjudiCLAIMS generates the letter content and saves it to the claim file, but it does not send the letter for you. You are responsible for sending the generated letter through your organization's normal correspondence process (email, mail, fax). After sending, you should update the relevant deadline (for example, marking the 15-day acknowledgment as met after sending the acknowledgment letter).

**Q: Can I use AdjudiCLAIMS to look up information about a claim that is not assigned to me?**
A: If you are a Claims Examiner, you can only access claims assigned to you. If you need information about another claim, ask your supervisor. Supervisors and administrators can access all claims within the organization.

**Q: Why does the system sometimes show "stub data" for MTUS matching?**
A: MTUS guideline matching uses a connection to the regulatory Knowledge Base. When the Knowledge Base is not connected (for example, in certain environments), the system provides realistic example data based on ACOEM guidelines for common body parts. The system clearly labels this as stub data so you know to verify against the official MTUS guidelines.

**Q: How often should I check my compliance dashboard?**
A: We recommend checking your dashboard at the start of each workday. The dashboard shows approaching deadlines sorted by urgency, so you can prioritize your work accordingly. Supervisors should review team metrics at least weekly to identify examiners who may need support.

**Q: What does the DOI Audit Readiness Score mean for my organization?**
A: The score is a self-assessment tool, not an official DOI rating. A score of 90-100 suggests strong audit preparedness. A score of 70-89 indicates areas for improvement. Below 70 suggests significant compliance gaps that should be addressed before an audit. The score breakdown shows exactly which areas need attention.

**Q: Can I export reports from AdjudiCLAIMS?**
A: Compliance reports (CCR 10101, CCR 10103, Deadline Adherence, and Audit Readiness) are generated within the system and can be produced for auditors. Work with your administrator to generate and provide reports as needed.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of AdjudiCLAIMS

1. **Upload documents as soon as you receive them.** The sooner documents are in the system, the sooner the AI can classify them, extract data, and auto-complete investigation items. This also ensures your claim file is up to date for audit purposes.

2. **Use specific questions in the AI chat.** Instead of "Tell me about this claim," ask specific questions like "What body parts are listed on the QME report?" or "What is the TD payment due date for the next cycle?" Specific questions get better, more cited answers.

3. **Start workflows early.** Do not wait until a deadline is approaching to start the relevant workflow. Start the "New Claim Intake" workflow the same day you create the claim. Start the "Coverage Determination" workflow as soon as your investigation is substantially complete.

4. **Pay attention to YELLOW deadlines.** GREEN means you have time; YELLOW means you should start working on it now. If you wait until RED, you are at risk of missing the deadline.

5. **Use the benefit calculator before issuing payments.** Even if you are experienced with TD calculations, running the numbers through the calculator creates an auditable record of the calculation inputs and results. If an auditor questions a payment, you can point to the calculator output.

6. **Dismiss Tier 1 terms as you learn them.** The education overlays are there to help you learn, not to slow you down. Once you know what AWE means, dismiss it. The system remembers your preferences.

7. **Review your monthly compliance summary.** The monthly review highlights missed deadlines and stale claims. Addressing these promptly prevents small issues from becoming audit findings.

8. **When in doubt, ask the AI chat.** If you are not sure about a regulatory requirement, a deadline calculation, or a procedural step, ask the chat. If it is a factual question, you will get a cited answer. If it turns out to be a legal question, the system will tell you and offer to connect you with counsel.
