Executive Summary California Workers' Compensation · AI Claims Management
Prepared: April 2026 Decision Maker Brief

Bottom Line Up Front

  • 1 What it is: An AI-powered claims management information tool for California Workers' Compensation claims examiners — combining a productivity assistant, a compliance engine, and an embedded training system in a single platform.
  • 2 What it saves: 1.5–2 hours per examiner per week through AI-assisted document review, auto-populated claim fields, and instant statutory benefit calculations — while projecting >98% regulatory deadline compliance across your entire portfolio.
  • 3 What it protects: Claims examiners are not attorneys. Every AI output is UPL-filtered through three enforcement layers. Legal questions are blocked and redirected to defense counsel. The system cannot provide legal advice — by architectural design, not just policy.

Compliance Posture

UPL · Enforced

Unauthorized Practice of Law Prevention

Three independent enforcement layers: pre-chat query classifier (regex + LLM), constrained system prompts, post-generation output validator (24+ prohibited patterns). 11 of 12 acceptance criteria verified by automated tests. 12th pending outside counsel sign-off.

HIPAA · BAAs Executed

HIPAA Compliance

Business Associate Agreements executed with all PHI sub-processors (Google, Anthropic). All AI providers contractually prohibited from training on client data. 7-year data retention. Breach notification procedure documented.

SOC 2 · Controls in Place

SOC 2 Security Controls

Argon2id password hashing, TOTP MFA, account lockout, RBAC (3 roles), org-scoped multi-tenancy, immutable audit trail (60+ event types, 7-year retention), Sentry error monitoring, GCP Secret Manager for all credentials.

DOI · Audit-Ready Reports

Department of Insurance Compliance

CCR 10101 claim file summaries, CCR 10103 activity logs, deadline adherence statistics, audit readiness score. All regulatory deadlines cite their statutory authority. All benefit calculations show formulas and Labor Code citations.
